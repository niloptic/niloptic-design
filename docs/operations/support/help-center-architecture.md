# Help Center Architecture вҖ” Knowledge Base Content Structure

Last updated: 2026-03-04

---

## Overview

The NIL Optic Help Center is a structured, searchable knowledge base embedded within the product support system. Content is organized by category, tagged by role and sport, and surfaced contextually based on the user's current workflow. Basketball is the primary sport and receives dedicated content sections, including seasonal guides for March Madness, the transfer portal, and tournament bracket analysis.

## Role-Based Content Tagging

Every article carries one or more role tags that control visibility and search ranking within the help system.

### Tag Definitions

| Tag        | Label       | Color Token                  | Description                           |
|------------|-------------|------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| `ad`       | AD          | `--support-accent` (red-600) | Athletic Director                     |
| `agent`    | AGENT       | Blue-600                     | Agent / Agency Admin                  |
| `athlete`  | ATHLETE     | Green-600                    | Student-Athlete                       |
| `coach`    | COACH       | Amber-600                    | Head Coach / Assistant Coach          |
| `fan`      | FAN         | Zinc-400                     | Fan / Public Viewer                   |

### Sport Tags

| Tag        | Label       | Description                           |
|------------|-------------|---------------------------------------|
| `bb`       | Basketball  | Basketball-specific content           |
| `fb`       | Football    | Football-specific content             |
| `all`      | All Sports  | Applies across all supported sports   |

### Tagging Rules

1. Every article must have at least one role tag and one sport tag.
2. Articles tagged `All` for roles appear for every user but are ranked lower than role-specific content.
3. Basketball-tagged articles are boosted in search results for users whose primary sport context is basketball.
4. Role tags drive the "For You" section in the help drawer home view.
5. Articles can have multiple role tags (e.g., an article relevant to both AD and Coach).

## Search Indexing Strategy

### Indexed Fields

| Field           | Weight | Notes                                          |
|-----------------|--------|-------------------------------------------------|
| `title`         | 10x    | Highest weight вҖ” primary match surface           |
| `summary`       | 5x     | High weight вҖ” concise answer                     |
| `tags`          | 4x     | Category, role, sport identifiers                |
| `body`          | 2x     | Full-text body content                           |
| `category`      | 3x     | Category name for broad filtering                |
| `related_articles` | 1x  | Title text of related articles                   |

### Ranking Boosters

| Booster             | Multiplier | Condition                                     |
|---------------------|------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Role match          | 1.5x       | Article role tag matches user's current role   |
| Sport match         | 1.3x       | Article sport tag matches user's sport context |
| Recency             | 1.2x       | Articles updated within last 30 days           |
| Popularity          | 1.1x       | Articles with > 100 views in last 7 days       |
| Difficulty match    | 1.1x       | Beginner articles boosted for new users        |

### Search Analytics

Track and report the following search metrics:

| Metric                  | Definition                                         | Target       |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|--------------|
| Total queries/day       | Number of search queries per day                   | Trending up  |
| Zero-result rate        | % of queries returning no results                  | < 5%         |
| Click-through rate      | % of searches where user clicks a result           | > 60%        |
| Average position clicked| Mean rank of the clicked result                    | < 3          |
| Query-to-ticket rate    | % of searches that lead to a support ticket        | < 10%        |
| Top 20 queries          | Most frequent search terms this period             | Weekly review|
| Unresolved query log    | Queries with no clicks and no follow-up            | Weekly review|

### Search Index Maintenance

- Full re-index: triggered on article publish/update
- Incremental index: every article edit queues a partial re-index within 5 minutes
- Synonym dictionary: maintained by support team (e.g., "value" = "valuation" = "worth" = "NIL price")
- Typo tolerance: fuzzy matching with edit distance of 2
- Stop words: standard English stop words excluded from indexing

### Update Cadence

| Content Type              | Review Frequency | Trigger                            |
|---------------------------|------------------|------------------------------------|
| Getting Started guides    | Quarterly        | Feature releases, UI changes       |
| Feature-specific articles | Monthly          | Feature updates, bug fixes         |
| Compliance articles       | Monthly          | NCAA rule changes, state law updates|
| Seasonal content (BB)     | Annually         | Pre-season, March Madness, portal  |
| Troubleshooting articles  | Bi-weekly        | New bug reports, ticket patterns   |
| Billing/account articles  | Quarterly        | Pricing changes, plan updates      |

### Content Quality Standards

1. Every article must have a clear, question-based title when possible.
2. Summary must answer the question in 1-2 sentences.
3. Step-by-step instructions must reference specific UI elements by their design-system names.
4. Screenshots must be updated within 2 weeks of any related UI change.
5. Articles must include at least 2 related article links.
6. Troubleshooting tables must cover the top 3 issues from ticket data.
7. All articles must pass readability check (target: grade 8 reading level).
8. Basketball examples are the default; other sports used only when sport-specific.

### Content Gap Detection

- Weekly: review zero-result search queries for missing content.
- Weekly: review ticket categories to find recurring questions without articles.
- Monthly: cross-reference new feature releases against Help Center coverage.
- Quarterly: audit all articles for accuracy and relevance.
- Flag any article with > 50% "Not helpful" feedback rating for immediate review.

## Help Center Metrics

### Article-Level Metrics

| Metric                  | Definition                                         | Target         |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| Views / article / week  | Unique article page views                          | Track trending |
| Avg. time on article    | Seconds spent reading the article                  | > 45s          |
| Helpful rate            | % of users clicking "Helpful" (thumbs up)          | > 80%          |
| Not helpful rate        | % of users clicking "Not helpful"                  | < 15%          |
| Ticket-after-article    | % of users who file a ticket after reading          | < 8%           |
| Bounce rate             | % who leave Help Center after viewing one article  | < 40%          |
| Related click-through   | % who navigate to a related article                | > 20%          |

### System-Level Metrics

| Metric                  | Definition                                         | Target         |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| Self-service resolution | % of help sessions that do not result in a ticket  | > 70%          |
| Search deflection rate  | % of searches resolved without a ticket            | > 75%          |
| Search miss rate        | % of searches with zero results                    | < 5%           |
| CSAT per article        | Average satisfaction rating per article             | > 4.2 / 5      |
| KB coverage score       | % of ticket categories with at least 3 articles    | 100%           |
| Content freshness       | % of articles updated within last 90 days          | > 85%          |
| Video completion rate   | % of users who watch video to end                  | > 60%          |
